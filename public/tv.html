Watch Notflix on your LG TV

Two ways to get it — start watching in seconds in your TV browser, or install it as a proper app on your home screen.

Easiest · 10 seconds

Open it in your TV's web browser

On your LG TV, open the Web Browser app and go to the address below. It loads straight into the big-screen TV layout — use your remote's arrows + OK to navigate.

mittamovies.netlify.app/?tv=1

Tip: add it to your browser's bookmarks/favourites for one-tap access next time.

Best experience · ~5 min

Install Notflix as an app (red “N” on your home screen)

LG TVs let you side-load apps in Developer Mode. You only do this setup once. You won't need your LG password on the computer — only the on-screen passphrase the TV shows you.

  1. On your TV: install the Developer Mode app from the LG Content Store, open it, sign in with your LG account, then turn Dev Mode Status = ON and Key Server = ON.
    Write down the IP address and the 6-character passphrase it shows.
  2. On your computer: install Node.js, then the webOS CLI:
    npm install -g @webos-tools/cli
  3. Download the Notflix app file (.ipk):
    ⬇ Download notflix-webos.ipk
  4. Connect your TV — replace TV_IP with your TV's IP, run both lines, and enter the passphrase when asked:
    ares-setup-device --add notflixtv --info "host=TV_IP port=9922 username=prisoner"
    ares-novacom --device notflixtv --getkey
  5. Install & launch Notflix (run from the folder where you saved the .ipk):
    ares-install --device notflixtv notflix-webos.ipk
    ares-launch --device notflixtv com.notflix.app

Notflix now appears on your TV's home launcher with the red “N” icon. 🎉

⏱️ Heads up: LG side-loaded apps pause after ~50 hours and the TV renews them automatically as long as the Developer Mode app stays installed and signed in. This is an LG limitation for all dev-mode apps.
